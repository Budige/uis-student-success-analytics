# UIS Student Success Analytics — OIRE Findings Summary
**Prepared for:** Office of Institutional Research and Effectiveness  
**Data Period:** 2014–2023 (IPEDS)  
**UIS Unit ID:** 145813

---

## Executive Summary

UIS enrollment stabilized in 2022–2023 after a 7-year decline, with online
programs now representing 56% of enrollment — the highest among all Illinois
public universities. Retention and graduation rates show steady improvement,
though equity gaps persist for Pell grant and first-generation students.

---

## Finding 1: Enrollment Stabilization (Positive)
- Total enrollment: 4,402 (2023), up +1.4% from 2022 trough of 4,298
- Online-only students grew from 1,942 (2014) to 2,468 (2023)
- **Recommendation**: Continue online program expansion as strategic differentiator

## Finding 2: Graduation Rate Improvement (Positive, More Work Needed)
- 6-year rate: 40.0% → 43.6% (+3.6pp over 7 cohorts)
- Trajectory: If trend continues, reaches 50% HLC benchmark ~2025 cohort
- **Recommendation**: Track 2018–2020 cohorts closely for COVID impact

## Finding 3: Retention Equity Gap (Critical Issue)
- Pell students retain at 65.8% vs. non-Pell at 76.1% → 10.3pp gap
- First-generation students: 66.4% vs. 77.4% → 11.0pp gap
- Part-time students: 53.2% vs. full-time 71.9% → 18.7pp gap
- **Recommendation**: Targeted advising and emergency aid programs for these groups

## Finding 4: Peer Benchmarking Position (Context)
- UIS ranks #5 of 9 IL public universities in graduation rate
- UIS ranks #4 in retention (above EIU, WIU, GSU, CSU)
- UIS leads in online enrollment (56.1%) — distinct market position
- **Recommendation**: Frame UIS as access institution with growing outcomes
